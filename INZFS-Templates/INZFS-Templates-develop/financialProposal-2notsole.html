Assessment criteria

Factors our assessors will consider include the following:

  • is your budget and breakdown realistic?
  • does the work you’ve planned match the predicted spend on the cost breakdown form?
  • is there a strong case for public money adding value?
  • does your project offer good value for money?
  • do the project costs provide fair market value?
  • have you made a convincing case for using sub-contractors instead of extra consortium partners? (if relevant)

Public finance limits

You can only claim a certain amount of public money towards your eligible project costs.

This amount depends on your organisation’s size, location and the type of work you’re doing.

Any money you claim through BEIS will form part of this total.

Public funding includes money from:

  • other government departments
  • UK public bodies
  • other governments or government organisations (From UK or elsewhere)

If you are applying for any other public funding, or are expecting to receive it, you need to tell us in your application.

If you’ve breached the rules, you’ll need to repay any money you’ve received, including interest.

You can find out how much public money you can claim towards your eligible project costs in question 6.1.

Match funding contributions

Your match funding contribution is the minimum amount you must pay towards your eligible project costs.

How much you need to pay depends on several factors.

These include:

  • the category of aid you’re applying for
  • your organisation’s size
  • whether you’re applying on your own or as part of consortium

You can find out your match funding contribution in question 6.1.

Completing the project cost breakdown form

We need you to fill in the project cost breakdown form to assess your application.

You’ll be asked to give information about your project’s costs.

This includes:

  • a breakdown of costs for each partner (if relevant)
  • labour and overheads
  • materials and equipment you’ll need
  • subcontractors
  • travel and subsistence
  • other costs

You can give extra information about your costs in the financial background section (question 6.3).

Assessment criteria

Factors our assessors will consider include, but are not limited to:

  • what is the basis of your calculations?
  • does your innovation save carbon directly, or will it be an enabler?
  • when can you get your innovation to market?
  • is it likely your innovation will be widely adopted?
  • when will your innovation start having an impact on 2050 net zero targets or the security of energy supply?
  • what is the likely impact over different timescales?
  • how does it compare to the benefits of similar technology?
  • does your technology lower the cost of carbon reduction for its target market?
  • is your project’s impact on 2050 targets or energy supply security attractive at its current development stage?
  • will your innovation have an impact on carbon targets or UK energy supply when on sale?

How your innovation will affect climate change or energy supply

Explain how your innovation will affect climate change or help secure the UK's energy supply.

For instance, the innovation may:

  • change users’ behaviour
  • reduce energy usage
  • lower the cost of maintenance or extend existing equipment's lifespan
  • improve existing materials' performance
  • help UK energy supply through generation or storage

Also state if there are any other competing developments which may improve on your innovation, if you’re aware of them.

How the grant will give BEIS value for money

Estimate how much CO2 could be saved in relation to the total grant you are applying for.

You should also explain how you calculated this number.

Potential reductions of carbon or greenhouse gas emissions

Estimate how much carbon or greenhouse gas could be reduced when your innovation is available.

You should base your prediction on:

  • carbon savings per unit
  • potential market size
  • sales you could expect

When your innovation will start to have an impact on climate change

Estimate when your innovation may start to affect climate change.

You should base this on:

  • when your innovation becomes available
  • how long it will take to be widely adopted
